The Space

The Game of Life is famous, but it's just one rule in a vast space of possibilities. Life-like cellular automata follow the same grid-based structure: cells live or die based on how many neighbors they have. Vary the birth and survival conditions, and you get entirely different universes.

With 9 possible neighbor counts (0-8), there are 218 = 262,144 distinct rules. Some produce stable patterns that freeze. Others oscillate endlessly. Some grow chaotic and noisy. And a rare few generate gliders, spaceships, and complex emergent structures.

Which ones? We can't predict. We have to run them and find out.

The Method

We systematically survey rule space, running each rule for 1000+ steps from random initial conditions and measuring:

λ
Lambda
Damage spreading. λ≈0 heals; λ>0 spreads chaos. Interesting rules sit at the boundary.
D
Dimension
Fractal dimension of patterns. D≈1 means filaments; D≈2 means solid blobs.
γ
Gamma
Activity rate. Does the pattern settle, oscillate, or keep changing?

Each measurement helps locate which regions of rule space produce complexity, and which collapse into order or chaos.

The Vision

Our goal isn't just to find beautiful patterns (though we've found many). It's to understand why certain rules produce complexity while others don't.

Extensible Analysis

Anyone can write new metrics and run them on the database. Each survey pass adds more metadata, enabling deeper filtering and discovery.

Layered Exploration

Filter → Analyze → Filter deeper → Analyze deeper. Multiple passes narrow focus and increase depth to find the gems.

Topology as Variable

How does the shape of space affect emergence? Flat grid, torus, sphere, hyperbolic — each creates different possibilities.

Patterns on Patterns

Hierarchical emergence: cells → still lifes → gliders → guns → computers. Understanding what enables each level.

By systematically varying the fundamental rules of the universe — birth/survival conditions, topology, grid tiling — we can understand how these design choices affect what kinds of complexity can exist.
